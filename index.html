I am a Persian-American, Silicon Valley born and raised. My training is in EECS from UC Berkeley; my primary research interest is brain-machine interfaces. I believe the hard problem of consciousness is the most important question worth asking. I also believe that direct augmentation of experience will be the greatest prize of this century.

I am currently researching photonics devices at UC Berkeley, Ming C. Wu Lab. I believe that a combination of MEMS process, analog circuits, and cell engineering will enable the next generation BMI. I previously interned at Rikky Muller Lab (UCB), Ritchie Chen Lab (UCSF), and .Neurorestore (EPFL). I have been making electronics since I was five and programming since I was 10. In my free time, I fly single engine aircraft based out of KOAK and KPAO. I study languages, and I currently speak French, Persian, Georgian, Russian, and Norwegian. I enjoy bikepacking and building bikes, and I enjoy diving as well. I play jazz trombone, and I actively study setar and singing in the Persian Radif tradition. I enjoy casual photography, reading, writing essays, and cooking.
